The six families

Six useful signals, not six absolute truths.

Large aroma datasets sort cannabis into roughly six groups. Each one is anchored on a terpene the lab already measures, so you can read a panel without memorising chemistry.

Citrus

Limonene

Sharp lemon peel and orange zest with a clean bitter edge.

Read the citrus page

Earthy

Myrcene

Damp soil and clove with a sweet, overripe-fruit edge.

Read the earthy page

Spice

β-Caryophyllene

Cracked black pepper and clove over warm wood.

Read the spice page

Pine

α-Pinene

Pine needle and rosemary, resinous and cool.

Read the pine page

Floral

Linalool

Read the floral page

Herbal

Terpinolene

Fresh-cut herbs and apple skin with a whisper of citrus.

Read the herbal page

We won't predict your high.

These families describe what a jar smells like. The evidence linking terpenes to effects is not settled, so NOSE stops where the evidence stops.
